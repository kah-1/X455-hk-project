About us

Founded in 2022, Tangerine Farm Flowers is floral design studio based in California's Bay Area. Known for its merging of an organic aesthetic with dark florals and imagery, the studio's work uses local materials and champions lesser known varieties and heritage breeds.

We pride ourselves on our sustainable practices. We are dedicated stewards of the land and committed to helping nature thrive so that humanity can thrive. We cultivate around the City of Berkeley and the Bay Area region to bring you nature-centered solutions. We are always looking forward becoming better gardeners and environmentalists to connect you and our nautral world without bring it harm. In essence, we are advocates of the plant world and we continually acknowledge that we operate gardens that sit sits on the territory of xučyun (Huichin), the ancestral and unceded land of the Chochenyo speaking Ohlone people, the successors of the sovereign Verona Band of Alameda County. This land was and continues to be of great importance to the Muwekma Ohlone Tribe and other familial descendants of the Verona Band.

Get to know more about our partner for sustainable packaging and practices.




Although there are officially five of us in the studio as Tangerine Farm Flowers colleagues, we are honored to be part of a far wider network in the Bay Area. We could not operate to our full standards without the help and guidance of fellow NorCal floral studios, farm workers, our friends at the Ferry Building and Ferry Building Farmers Market, horticultural fellows, and community gardeners. We also draw inspiration from friends across the country.

When you book an appointment with us, you may be met by Anna, our founder; Susan, our COO; Rose, our Sustainability Expert; Cat, our on-site floral arranger; and Bria, our off-site floral arranger. The entire team is instrumental to the design and brand ethos of Tangerine Farm Flowers, and we're lucky that all five of us have been a part of TFF since the early concept phase. With shared backgrounds in visual design, horticulture, and local flora, the team is empowered to bring to life your vision without negatively impacting our local and global environments. We constantly push each other to bring forth our best ideas for our clients and the Bay Area community. To ensure this sustainability, we are constantly working with the UC Berkeley's Botanical Garden to offer classes and large-scale project assistance.

Our process

When you book an appointment with us, you may be met by Anna, our founder; Susan, our COO; Rose, our Sustainability Expert; Cat, our on-site floral arranger; and Bria, our off-site floral arranger. We'll be there for you every step of the way, whether you're looking for a one-time arrangement or assistance on a wide-scale project. Our main goal is that we marry your vision with sustainable practices that ensures the longevity of the land on which we grow our flowers and plants. We also strive to curate with California native plants.

We strive to source from farmers within California who, one, grow native plants, two, operate ethically, and three, operate sustainably. We also only use packaging and materials from businesses whose ideals align with ours. To get a list of potential sources that we would use for your specific project, please do not hesitate to email us. We're more than happy to talk about our partners and business goals!
